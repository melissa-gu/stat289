Puffed grain is the result of a process developed by Dr. Alexander P. Anderson of New York City in 1902. It was first manufactured by American Cereal Co. (which became Quaker Oats Co. ) and was introduced at the St. Louis World's Fair in 1904 as puffed rice . Distinct from popcorn , which naturally pops and puffs itself with heating, puffed grain is created by placing whole grains under high pressure with steam . When the containment vessel's seal is suddenly broken, the entrained steam then flashes and bloats the endosperm of the kernel , increasing its volume to many times its original size.
