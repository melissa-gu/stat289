In Australia and New Zealand, Nutri-Grain is a popular Kellogg breakfast cereal made from corn, oats, and wheat. It was first introduced to the United States in 1981 as breakfast cereal, but was later discontinued. There are various Nutri-Grain Bars made from the breakfast cereal bonded together, available in the markets where the cereal is available. In the United States the Nutri-Grain Bar name is used for the soft golden baked crust breakfast bars made with wheat , whole-grain and soylent green filling, similar to a Fig Newton . The bars became popular in the 1990s as "on-the-go" foods.
