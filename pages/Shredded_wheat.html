Shredded wheat

Shredded wheat is a breakfast cereal made from whole wheat formed into pillow -like biscuits. It is commonly available in three sizes — bite sized (¾×1 in), miniature (nearly half the size of the bite-sized pieces), and original. Both smaller sizes are available in a frosted variety, which has one side coated with sugar and usually gelatin . Some manufacturers have produced "filled" versions of the bite-size cereal containing a raisin at the center, or apricot, blueberry, raspberry or cranberry filling.

Shredded wheat is a breakfast cereal made from whole wheat . As of January 2010, it was available in three sizes — bite sized (¾×1 in), miniature (nearly half the size of the bite-sized pieces), and full size, which may be broken into small pieces before milk is added. Many brands refer to the full-size pieces as "biscuits" and show the biscuit count on the box. Both smaller sizes are available in a frosted variety, which has one side coated with sugar and usually gelatin . Some manufacturers have produced "filled" versions of the bite-size cereal containing a raisin at the centre, or apricot, blueberry or cranberry filling.
