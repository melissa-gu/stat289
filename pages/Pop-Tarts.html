Pop-Tarts

Pop-Tarts is a brand of toaster pastries that the Kellogg Company introduced in 1964. Pop-Tarts have a sugary filling sealed inside two layers of thin, rectangular pastry crust. Most varieties are also frosted . Although sold pre-cooked, they are designed to be warmed inside a toaster or microwave oven . They are usually sold in pairs inside Mylar (previously foil) packages and do not require refrigeration.

A Pop-Tart is a flat rectangular toaster pastry approximately 3 in (75 mm ) by 5.5 in (115mm), made by the Kellogg Company . Pop-Tarts have a sugary filling sealed inside two layers of rectangular, thin pastry crust; each layer of this crust is about 0.1in (2mm) thick. Most varieties have frosting , but some do not. They can be eaten without being warmed, but are often warmed inside a toaster . (More recently, certain varieties have been marketed as tasting best when left to cool in the freezer .) They are usually sold in pairs inside foil packages, and do not require refrigeration. [1]
